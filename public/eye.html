Our Machines and Eye Tests

Visual Acuity

We have an automated/electric visual acuity chart that tests the resolving power of the eye or simply the ability of the eye to distinguish small differences in a uniform background.

Refraction

The autorefractormeter/keratometer, the automated phoropter and trial lens box are all used to determine the dioptric power of the eye in order to get accurate glass prescription.

Biomicroscopy

The instrument used for this procedure is the slit lamp biomicroscope, it consists of a high intensity light source that can be focused to shine a thin sheet of light into the eye. It facilitates examination of the anterior and posterior segments of the eye. The test provides a stereoscopic magnified view of the eye structures in detail, enabling anatomical diagnosis to be made for a variety of eye conditions.

Tonometry

This is performed using the Non-contact tonometer (NCT), to measure the intra ocular pressure of the eye. It is a very important test in the evaluation of patients at risk of glaucoma.

Perimetry

Done using the Visual Field Analyzer. In this test, a topographic 'map' is created of the visual field to diagnose and evaluate diseases of the optic nerve (e.g glaucoma), retina and other diseases that connect with the brain (e.g tumors).

Pachymetry

The A-Scan machine measures the thickness of the cornea and by so doing helps to determine the true intraocular pressure of a patient's eye.

Fundoscopy

This test done with the Ophthalmoscope is part of routine physical examination, it is used to see inside the fundus of the eye and other structures.

Colour Vision Test

Done with the Ishihara test chart, to measure patient's ability to tell the differences among colours to rule out colour blindness and anomalies.

Neutralization

The automated forcimeter verifies the correct prescription in a pair of glasses, helps to properly orient and mark uncut lenses and to confirm the correct mounting of lenses in spectacle frames.

Retina Imaging

Also known as fundus photography and done with a Fundus camera, it involves capturing a photograph of the back of the eye, i.e the fundus, to visualize and assess the central and peripheral retina, optic disc and macula.

Fundus Scan

This is a non-invasive imaging test done with the Optical Coherence Tomography machine (OCT). The test uses light waves to take cross section pictures of the retina and optic nerve. The machine sees each of the retinas distinctive layers and allows to measure their thickness thereby helping to detect even 10% damage done in a variety of eye diseases.

Glass Dispensing

This is a non-invasive imaging test done with the Optical Coherence Tomography machine (OCT). The test uses light waves to take cross section pictures of the retina and optic nerve. The machine sees each of the retinas distinctive layers and allows to measure their thickness thereby helping to detect even 10% damage done in a variety of eye diseases.
